# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Two distinct groups:

- **Attendees**: Event-goers buying tickets on their mobile phones via M-Pesa STK Push. They are on the move, often in low-connectivity areas, and need a fast, frictionless checkout. Their emotional state is anticipation and excitement about the event.
- **Admin staff**: Gate and box-office personnel verifying tickets at the event entrance. They need to scan QR codes and look up tickets in real time, under time pressure, sometimes in direct sunlight. Their primary need is speed and certainty — admit or reject, instantly.

## Product Purpose

Premium mobile-first ticketing, verification, and checkout platform for the GOODLIFE event. Handles the complete lifecycle: ticket browsing and purchase (M-Pesa), PDF ticket generation and WhatsApp delivery, QR-code gate scanning, and an admin dashboard for sales metrics and ticket management. Success means attendees get through the gate fast and organizers see a real-time, accurate sales ledger.

## Brand Personality

Bold, Premium, Confident. The brand carries additional dimensions of energetic/modern (it's an event, not a bank) and luxury/exclusive/refined (it's GOODLIFE, not a generic party). The voice is direct, uppercase, declarative — no hedging, no soft CTAs.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS/corporate**: Avoid cream-and-blue shadcn/ui default aesthetics, soft rounded corners, flat cards, standard dashboard templates. This is not a B2B SaaS.
- **Dark/neon/cyberpunk**: No dark themes with electric accents. GOODLIFE is a daytime / high-energy premium event, not a nightclub or gaming platform.
- **Minimalist/artsy**: Avoid excessive whitespace, thin type, understated elegance, or "breathing room" for its own sake. The interface should feel substantial and packed with information.

## Design Principles

1. **Confidence, not caution** — Every button, badge, and label commits. No ghost buttons, no skeleton loaders, no half-opened states. The UI is decisive because the gate is decisive.
2. **Heavy is the point** — Thick borders, hard block shadows, bold uppercase weights. The interface has physical presence. Nothing floats, nothing feels ephemeral.
3. **Event energy, not corporate utility** — This is a ticket to a real gathering, not a SaaS subscription. The design carries the excitement of the event itself through color, weight, and rhythm.
4. **Thumbs and sunlight** — Both flows (buying on mobile, scanning at the gate) happen in challenging conditions. Controls are large, contrast is high, decisions are binary and obvious.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific WCAG level mandated beyond basic readability. High contrast is already a design requirement (see principles 2 and 4).
